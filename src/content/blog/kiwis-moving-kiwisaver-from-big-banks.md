## The big shift is happening

Something interesting is going on in the KiwiSaver world: Kiwis are moving their money away from the big banks — and in serious numbers.

According to [RNZ](https://www.rnz.co.nz/news/business/568881/kiwisaver-shakeup-sees-billions-shifted-from-big-banks-to-boutique-operators), billions of dollars have been shifted from the major banks to boutique and specialist KiwiSaver providers. It's a trend that's been building for years, and it's picking up pace.

## Why are people leaving the banks?

The short answer: returns. When people actually compare how their bank's KiwiSaver fund has performed against specialist providers, the numbers often tell a stark story.

A 2-3% difference in annual returns might not sound like much. But over 20 or 30 years, compounding turns that small gap into hundreds of thousands of dollars. That's the difference between a comfortable retirement and a tight one.

The big banks have massive customer bases — often because people signed up for KiwiSaver at the same time they opened a bank account and never thought about it again. But "set and forget" is proving to be an expensive default for many Kiwis.

## What the boutique providers are doing differently

Specialist KiwiSaver providers tend to be laser-focused on one thing: investment performance. They don't have the overhead of running a bank, and many have built their entire business around delivering strong returns after fees.

That focus is showing up in the numbers. Over the last 10 years, several boutique providers have consistently outperformed the big banks across multiple fund categories.

## What does this mean for you?

If your KiwiSaver is still sitting with the bank you signed up with years ago, it's worth asking one simple question: **how have your returns compared to the rest of the market?**

You might be in a great spot. But if you're not, the cost of doing nothing adds up fast.

## The bottom line

More and more Kiwis are waking up to the fact that where their KiwiSaver sits matters — a lot. The shift away from big banks isn't about loyalty or brand. It's about results.
